Hello,

I am a designer passionate about a lot of things.

I learned architecture at school. I have been working as a graphic designer, a web designer, a museum docent, a videographer, and a UI/UX designer.

Started with architecture, I learned to think about design critically.

Besides all the technical and visual design skills, an important thing I learned from architecture is the design thinking process. From developing projects from start to end, I learned how to clarify the goal at the beginning, identify the problems during the process, and how to make decisions to push forward.

UX as my new passion.

What leads me to become interested in UX is my last summer intern experience. It broadened my understanding of design: it is not just what it looks like, it is the mechanism to make it work and the empathy to think for people. Through projects with real clients, I practiced design as a method, and space as a medium to influence the way people live, work, eat and interact with each other.

Always willing to learn.

With curiosity and passion for the field of technology, I self-learned HTML, CSS, javascript, and python. I have made websites and some front-end prototypes from scratch. When I began to do some UI projects, the coding background enables me to better understand how art and technology come together.

In conclusion, I want to become a thoughtful and considerate designer, who is willing to listen to people's need, prompt to find ways to solve problems, and enthusiastic to make an impact.
